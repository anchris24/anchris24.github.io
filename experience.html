Predicting Nanoparticle Toxicity Through Machine Learning-Based Models

Research Field: Machine Learning
Role: Research Intern, Summer Undergraduate Research Internship
Affiliation: Houston Methodist Research Institute
Dates: May 2023 - June 2025 (Internship period: May 2023 - August 2023)
Tools Used: Python, Scikit-learn, SHAP (Feature Selection, Explainability), Statistical Analysis

  • Developed 32 ML classifiers (XGBoost, Gradient Boosting, CatBoost), training on curated dataset of 2,896 samples with 19 features (expanded to over 8,000 post-internship) to predict toxicity at 91.2% accuracy.
  • Implemented nested 10-fold cross-validation, GridSearch hyperparameter tuning, and SHAP explainability analysis to ensure robust model performance and interpretability.
  • Quantified model generalizability through independent testing (433 samples), demonstrating robust performance (88.7% accuracy) with selected key features, enabling safer nanoparticle design.

Dates: September 2021 - March 2022
